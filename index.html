Overview of the PPL Routine

The Push Pull Legs (PPL) routine is a popular and effective split for building muscle and strength by grouping exercises based on movement patterns. It allows for adequate recovery and efficient training.

DayWorkout TypeFocus
MondayPushChest, Shoulders, Triceps
TuesdayPullBack, Biceps
WednesdayLegsQuads, Hamstrings, Calves
ThursdayRest or Repeat—

This routine can be cycled 2x/week or customized based on recovery. PPL is great for intermediate to advanced lifters looking to increase volume and intensity.

Refine Your Strength and Physique

This section dives into advanced weight training techniques such as supersets, drop sets, rest-pause sets, and tempo manipulation to challenge your muscles and break through plateaus.

  • Supersets: Combine two exercises back to back with no rest to increase intensity.
  • Drop Sets: Perform a set to failure, then reduce the weight and continue.
  • Rest-Pause: Break a heavy set into small rest intervals to complete more reps.
  • Tempo Training: Control rep speed to increase time under tension and muscle engagement.

These techniques are best suited for experienced lifters and should be integrated carefully to avoid overtraining.
